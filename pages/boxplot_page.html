Figure 8. This plot shows an interactive boxplot of the salary distribution between job types.

Students are often interested in comparing the financial aspects of data analysis, data science, and machine learning roles. The boxplot reveals that machine learning roles have the highest median and spread of compensation, while data analysis has the lowest median. Data science falls in between, but interestingly has more outliers, likely due to the companies hiring. By hovering over the outliers, we can see what company names those data points refer to; they include SAIC, Reddit, and Dave Inc., which may offer higher salaries than other companies because SAIC and Reddit are large companies in their respective industries, while Dave Inc. is a financial technology firm that values its data analyst hires.

DSAN Students may also be curious to see how compensation differs across these three job types between the DC area and the rest of the United States. The boxplots reveals that the median for machine learning salaries are still higher than data analysis and data science roles despite comparing US and DC job oppenings. The data analysis compensation median for DC is much lower than the US but it does have a wider spread. This may be due to the high concentration of non-profits in DC looking to hire analysts.
