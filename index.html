Introduction

It is no question that jobs in the technology industry are a large part of the U.S. job market. In fact, net technology employment accounts for 7.9% of the total U.S. workforce [1]. Furthermore, the growth rate of the technology industry has seen a significant increase since the COVID-19 pandemic. For example, 2019 saw a growth rate of technology jobs of 0.1% while the most recent figure has jumped to 2% [2]. That is a 1900% increase!

When dividing the numerous sectors the technology industry has to offer and analyzing their growth, roles that might be of particular interest to students in the Georgetown's Master's of Data Science program include, but are not limited to, data analysis, data science and machine learning. The Bureau of Labor Statistics predicts 23%, 36% and 21% in employment growth rate for 2021-2031 in those fields respectively. However, what's really the difference between these three technology job types?

There have been many times when family and friends asked the same question, or rather, "what does a data scientist do exactly?" I ask the same question myself sometimes; especially when starting the job search process since many companies post applications for these roles that indeed have very similar descriptions. This project has presented the perfect opportunity to dive deeper into the technology job market by visually analyzing web crawling results for data analysis, data science and machine learning roles posted by companies looking to hire.
